Shaping the Spatio-Temporal Structure of Event-Based Camera Streams for Robot Control (Master Thesis)

The aim of this project is to attain constrained motions by shaping the spatio-temporal structure of event-based camera streams. The algorithm actively uses the movement of the robot to assess the shaping of the stream’s structure. The achieved constrained motions are bio-inspired by natural vision systems. Developed in Python/C++ and ROS. (Not graded yet)

Distance estimation using fixation on a 2DoF robot (University Project)

[PDF]

This project aims to explore the potential of active perception for predicting depth information within a scene. It specifically focuses on replicating a bio-inspired mechanism called fixation using a 2-DoF robot. Fixation involves keeping a feature centered in the image frame while moving, thereby revealing the 3D properties of the scene.
